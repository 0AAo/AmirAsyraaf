The Apple Lisa at 40, and 6 more Apple tech flubs that led to better things

The Apple Lisa has turned 40 and was designed by Steve Jobs to be the next stage of computing. It featured a desktop manager, folders for documents, a wastebasket for work that didn't turn out well, a mouse for controlling an on-screen pointer, and a document-centric workflow. However, when MacWorks allowed the Lisa to run Mac apps, it had a reputation for sluggishness and crashing under heavy load, and sales were poor. When Jobs was booted off the team, he pilfered its best bits for the Mac, which on release blazed past its forebear. Jobs later said Apple had been blinded by corporate sales and betrayed its roots, but the Lisa still gets a rough deal as everyone forgets how much of Apple's later success originated from this beige innovator.
